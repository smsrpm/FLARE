# PURPOSE AND SCOPE

FLARE (Fast Licensing Accident Response Engine) is a reduced-order reactor safety analysis code for rapid evaluation of design-basis and selected beyond-design-basis transients in pressurized-water reactors and small modular reactors. It is not a replacement for production licensing codes such as RELAP5, TRACE, CATHARE, or ATHLET. Its purpose is to provide a transparent, computationally affordable, single-node analytical framework for understanding the dominant mass, energy, power, fuel-temperature, source-term, and containment-response trends governing light-water reactor accident progression.

FLARE supports the following transient and accident categories: large-break and small-break LOCA, reactivity-initiated accidents (rod ejection, rod withdrawal), anticipated transients without scram (ATWS), loss of forced circulation, and scenarios involving containment response to mass, energy, and hydrogen releases.

The principal calculated figures of merit are:
- Peak cladding temperature (PCT) — compared against the 10 CFR 50.46 limit of 1204 deg C
- Maximum local oxidation (MLO) — equivalent cladding reacted fraction from Baker-Just
- Core-wide hydrogen generation — total zirconium-steam reaction, used for containment assessment
- RCS pressure and inventory — overpressure and depressurization trends
- FLARECON containment pressure and temperature — dry-containment margin
- Radiological dose at EAB, LPZ, and control room — source-term and dose screening


# CODE ARCHITECTURE

The FLARE code system consists of:
- flare_sim.py — primary RCS simulation engine; function pwr_sim() reads an Excel input workbook and produces Excel and CSV output
- flarecon_sim.py — containment response engine; run_flarecon() and run_integrated_flarecon() driven by FLARE RCS output
- flare_ui.py — PWR Simulator user interface
- flare_ua.py / flare_ua_worker.py — uncertainty analysis interface and durable worker
- flare_risk.py — risk assessment tool
- flare_model_editor.py, flare_analyzer.py — pre/post-processing tools

The simulation uses an operator-split strategy: thermal-hydraulics, kinetics, and cladding temperature are solved in sequence within each time step with one-step lagging at coupling interfaces.


# THERMAL-HYDRAULIC SYSTEM MODEL

## Modeling Basis

The thermal-hydraulic model uses the homogeneous equilibrium model (HEM). Liquid and vapor share a common velocity and temperature. The RCS is treated as a single well-mixed control volume with uniform pressure P and specific enthalpy h. Water and steam properties come from the IAPWS-IF97 formulation via XSteamPython.

## Mass Balance

dM/dt = m_in - m_break - m_PORV - m_letdown

M is updated explicitly each time step. Equilibrium quality x_eq = (h - h_f) / h_fg drives property selection and heat-transfer regime.

## Energy and Pressure-Enthalpy Update

The energy and volume conservation equations form a coupled 2x2 linear system in the pressure and enthalpy increments. The coefficient matrix A contains partial derivatives of specific internal energy e and specific volume v with respect to P and h evaluated by finite differences. The system is solved by numpy.linalg.solve() at each time step. This semi-implicit approach avoids iterative nonlinear solution while providing good numerical stability.

## Break Flow Model

Break area: A_break = pi * d_break^2 / 4

For subcooled conditions: Bernoulli model, G = sqrt(2 * rho_l * (P_RCS - P_back))

For saturated/flashing conditions: Moody-type equilibrium critical-flow model. The critical mass flux G_crit is found by maximizing G over throat pressure P_c. Three slip options are supported: homogeneous equilibrium (S=1), Moody slip (S = (v_g/v_f)^(1/3)), Fauske slip (S = (v_g/v_f)^(1/2)). Fauske is the default for LOCA.

The actual break flow is: m_break = C_d * A_break * G_crit

Separate discharge coefficients C_d,sub and C_d,sat are used for subcooled and two-phase conditions (default 0.9 for both). When P_back/P_RCS exceeds the critical pressure ratio (default 0.55), flow transitions to subcritical.

## PORV Model

The PORV opens when RCS pressure exceeds its setpoint. Relief flow uses the same ERM critical-flow model as the break. A predictor-corrector method pins pressure at the setpoint by adjusting the PORV flow fraction. Pressure is updated with under-relaxation factor P_relax to damp oscillations.

## Steam Generator Model

Two options selected by sg_table_flag:
- sg_table_flag = 0 (default): Q_SG = UA_eff * (T_primary - T_secondary). UA_eff scales with primary flow rate.
- sg_table_flag = 1: Q_SG prescribed from column D of the time-series table. Positive = normal heat removal; negative = overcooling (e.g. MSLB).

## RCP Coastdown

Pump speed decays as omega(t) = omega_rated * exp(-t / tau_pump). Core coolant velocity v_core = m_pump / (rho_core * A_flow_core), where A_flow_core = N_pins * (pitch^2 - pi*d_pin^2/4).

## Collapsed Level Diagnostic

V_liquid = (1 - x_eq) * M / rho_f. Level = V_liquid / A_vessel. Used to identify core uncovery, reflood, and dry-core transitions.


# ACCUMULATOR AND ECCS MODELS

## Accumulator

Gas pressure follows a polytropic law: P_g = P_g0 * (V_g0 / V_g)^n. Injection begins when P_g exceeds RCS pressure plus check-valve pressure drop. Flow: m_acc = C_d * A_line * sqrt(2 * rho_w * (P_g - P_RCS - delta_P_check)).

## Pumped Safety Injection (HPSI and LPSI)

Each pump train delivers flow degraded linearly with RCS back-pressure from rated flow at favorable pressure to zero at shutoff head. Flow is available only after ESFAS actuation plus injection delay.

## CVCS Makeup/Letdown

Activated by cvcs_kgs > 0. Net RCS mass effect is zero (balanced makeup and letdown). Net boron effect per timestep: d(m_B)/dt|_CVCS = cvcs_kgs * (cvcs_boron_ppm - C_RCS) * 1e-6. Used for boron dilution cases; set cvcs_boron_ppm = 0 for unborated (worst-case dilution) and si_fill_threshold = 1.0 to disable ECCS pump degradation in intact-RCS conditions.


# CORE AND FUEL MODELS

## Rod-Bundle Geometry

Key defaults (user-overridable via input file — N_pins is not hardwired):
- N_pins = 18,200 fuel pins (default; read from input parameter N_pins)
- d_pin = 9.5 mm outer diameter
- pitch_pin = 12.6 mm square pitch
- L_heated = 2.4 m active length
- delta_clad = 0.57 mm cladding thickness
- r_fuel = 4.18 mm fuel pellet radius

Derived: SurfArea = N_pins * pi * d_pin * L_heated; D_h = 4 * A_flow / (N_pins * pi * d_pin); q_avg'' = Q_core / SurfArea; q_hot'' = F_r * F_z * q_avg''.

## Cladding Heat Structure

Implicit finite-difference scheme on a 1-D planar slab across cladding thickness delta_clad. Inner surface: prescribed heat flux from Q_total/SurfArea. Outer surface: convective boundary condition with coolant-side HTC alpha. Solves a tridiagonal system at each time step (unconditionally stable).

## Fuel Temperature

Quasi-steady thermal resistance chain: T_fuel_center - T_coolant = q_hot'' * (R_fuel + R_gap + R_clad + R_conv). R_fuel = r_fuel / (4*k_fuel) for a solid cylinder with uniform heat generation. First-order thermal lag with time constant tau_fuel = rho_fuel * cp_fuel * r_fuel^2 / (4 * k_fuel) captures stored fuel energy.

## Hot-Channel Model

T_hot_clad = T_avg_clad + (F_r * F_z - 1) * q_avg'' / alpha. PCT = max over time of T_hot_clad. The composite peaking factor F_hot = F_r * F_z sets the limiting local rod state.

## Convective Heat Transfer

Forced convection: Dittus-Boelter, Nu = 0.023 * Re^0.8 * Pr^0.4. Laminar fallback (Nu=3.66 at Re<2300) is suppressed for liquid-phase core HTC to prevent spurious natural-convection dominance during pump coastdown; it is retained for steam post-CHF cooling.

Natural convection (when flow is absent or degraded): Churchill-Chu correlation, Nu = [0.825 + 0.387*Ra^(1/6) / (1 + (0.492/Pr)^(9/16))^(8/27)]^2.

Controlling HTC: h_c = max(h_DB, h_NC) * f_HTC.

Post-CHF (film boiling): Simplified Bromley model, h_FB = C_B^(4/3) * q_avg''^(1/3). Three post-CHF model options via post_chf_model:
- bromley (default): direct Bromley HTC, effectively uncapped
- cold_leg: htc_post_chf capped at 50 W/m2K with under-relaxation; activates on DNBR<1, core uncovery, or at scram time when x_eq >= 0
- hot_leg: direct Bromley, no under-relaxation

Uncertainty scaling: htc_core_mult scales forced-convection HTC; htc_fb_mult scales film-boiling HTC.

Dry-core model (when vessel level falls below film_boiling_level_m): h_dry = h_gas + epsilon*sigma*(T_clad^4 - T_gas^4)/(T_clad - T_gas).

## Critical Heat Flux and DNBR

DNBR = q_CHF'' / q_hot''. CHF is the Biasi (1967) correlation blended with the modified Zuber pool boiling model. Biasi is primary at G > 200 kg/m2s; pure Zuber below 100 kg/m2s; linear blend between. Bowring (1972) is available as an alternative for high-pressure regime (P > ~13 MPa). DNBR < 1 triggers film boiling. Minimum DNBR (MDNBR) is tracked and reported.


# STATISTICAL ROD-STATE MODEL, HOT PIN, AND MEAN OXIDIZING ROD

FLARE supplements its lumped-parameter core model with a statistical rod-state reconstruction to estimate regulatory figures-of-merit that depend on local fuel-rod conditions.

## Rod-State Distribution

The population of N_rod fuel rods is modeled with a normal distribution in the peaking multiplier F_r*:
  F_r* ~ N(1, sigma_F^2)

sigma_F = (F_hot - 1) / k_sigma, where F_hot = F_r * F_z and k_sigma (default 3.0) is the number of standard deviations between the average rod and the hot pin. At k_sigma = 3.0, the hot pin is at the 99.87th percentile.

## Temperature Mapping

For a rod with multiplier F_r*, its cladding temperature is:
  T_clad(F_r*, t) = T_c(t) + F_r* * (T_clad(t) - T_c(t))

The hot-pin cladding temperature is T_hot(t) = T_c(t) + F_hot * (T_clad(t) - T_c(t)).

PCT = max over time of T_hot(t).

## Failed-Rod Fractions

For a failure threshold T_fail, the critical peaking multiplier is:
  F_crit(t) = (T_fail - T_c(t)) / (T_clad(t) - T_c(t))

The instantaneous failed fraction: f_fail(t) = P(F_r* >= F_crit(t)) = 1 - Phi((F_crit(t) - 1) / sigma_F)

Failed fractions are latched (never decrease). Two thresholds are tracked:
- Gap release: T_gap = 800 deg C (input: T_gap_release_C)
- Early in-vessel: T_EIV = 1204 deg C (input: T_early_iv_C)

## Mean Oxidizing Rod

The mean oxidizing rod represents the conditional mean temperature of rods above the oxidation threshold T_ox (default 800 deg C). The critical multiplier for oxidation is Fox_crit(t) = (T_ox - T_c(t)) / (T_clad(t) - T_c(t)). The oxidizing fraction is f_ox(t) = 1 - Phi(z_ox(t)) where z_ox = (Fox_crit - 1) / sigma_F.

The conditional mean multiplier of oxidizing rods:
  F_ox(t) = 1 + sigma_F * phi(z_ox(t)) / (1 - Phi(z_ox(t)))

Mean oxidizing rod temperature: T_mean_ox(t) = T_c(t) + F_ox(t) * (T_clad(t) - T_c(t))

The mean oxidizing rod is used for population-integrated oxidation and source-term escalation. The hot pin is used for peak local oxidation (MLO). These are distinct quantities and must not be confused.

## Figures-of-Merit from the Rod-State Model

PCT = max_t [T_hot(t)]
MLO = max_t [ECR_hot(t)]   (maximum local oxidation, hot-pin ECR)
ECR_mean_ox_max = max_t [ECR_mean_ox(t)]
f_gap_max = max_t [f_gap(t)]
f_EIV_max = max_t [f_EIV(t)]
M_H2_max = max_t [M_H2(t)]


# ZIRCONIUM OXIDATION AND HYDROGEN GENERATION

## Zirconium-Steam Reaction

Zr + 2 H2O -> ZrO2 + 2 H2 + Q_rxn

Stoichiometry: delta_m_Zr = delta_m_O * M_Zr / (2 * M_O); delta_m_H2 = delta_m_O * M_H2 / M_O.

## Baker-Just Parabolic Oxidation Law

dW/dt = K_BJ(T_clad) / W, where K_BJ(T) = A_BJ * exp(-B_BJ / T). W is oxygen areal weight gain. This gives parabolic behavior: oxidation slows as the oxide layer thickens.

Time-step update: W_n+1 = sqrt(W_n^2 + 2 * K_BJ(T^(n+1/2)) * delta_t)

Oxidation is suppressed below threshold temperature T_ox (modeling guard, not a physical statement).

## Equivalent Cladding Reacted Fraction

ECR_s (%) = 100 * W_s * M_Zr / (2 * M_O * rho_Zr * delta_clad)

Three rod states are integrated separately: hot pin (for MLO), mean oxidizing rod (for core-wide hydrogen and source-term escalation), and core average.

## Core-Wide Hydrogen Generation

Number of oxidizing rods: N_ox(t) = f_ox(t) * N_rod
Oxidizing cladding area per rod: A_clad = pi * d_pin * L_heated
Incremental hydrogen mass per step: delta_M_H2 = N_ox^(n+1/2) * A_clad * delta_W_mean_ox * M_H2 / M_O

The hydrogen source is passed to FLARECON as a containment gas source.

## Coupling to Source Term

Severe-event source-term escalation uses E_mean = max_t [ECR_mean_ox(t)] / 100 (expressed as a fraction). Release fractions interpolate between DBA values and severe-event targets using E_mean.


# POINT KINETICS AND REACTIVITY MODELS

## Point-Kinetics Equations

dP_n/dt = ((rho_net - beta) / Lambda) * P_n + sum_i [lambda_i * C_i]
dC_i/dt = (beta_i / Lambda) * P_n - lambda_i * C_i   (i = 1 to 6 groups)

Q_fission = P_n * Q_rated

## Reactivity Feedback

rho_net = rho_ext(t) + rho_D + rho_M + rho_v + rho_scram + rho_boron

Doppler: rho_D = alpha_D * (T_fuel - T_fuel,0)   [alpha_D negative, typically -2 to -4 pcm/deg C]

Moderator temperature: rho_M = alpha_M * (T_coolant - T_coolant,0) * (1 - alpha_void)^3

Void: rho_v = alpha_M_void * x_eq * 100   [per % mass steam quality]

Boron: rho_boron = alpha_boron_pcm_ppm * (C_RCS - C_init)

Scram: ramp insertion of trip_rod_worth_pcm over trip_rod_insertion_time_s

External (RIA cases): rho_ext(t) from column F of the time-series table.

## Scram Model

Five RPS setpoints monitored: high neutron flux (trip_power_frac, default 110%), low RCS pressure (trip_P_lo_kPa, default 90% of initial P), high RCS pressure (disabled by default), low coolant flow (disabled by default), timed manual trip (trip_time_s, default never). Signal processing delay trip_delay (default 1.5 s) before scram executes.

Post-scram power: six-group delayed neutron decay curve using actual rod worth in dollars (trip_rod_worth_pcm / beta_eff).

## Prompt Jump and Nordheim-Fuchs

Prompt Jump Approximation: P_new = P_old * (beta_eff - rho_prev) / (beta_eff - rho_net). Applied when both reactivities are sub-critical and the time step would under-resolve the power change.

For prompt-supercritical excursions (rho_net > beta_eff), the Nordheim-Fuchs analytic model integrates over N_sub = 1000 sub-steps within each master time step. The burst self-terminates when Doppler feedback consumes the excess reactivity.

## Decay Heat

ANS-5.1-1979 standard: Q_decay(t_rel) / Q_0 = sum_i [a_i * lambda_i * exp(-lambda_i * t_rel) * (1 - exp(-lambda_i * T_irr))] summed over 23 nuclide groups for U-235, U-238, and Pu-239 fission products. Actinide contributions (U-239, Np-239) added separately.


# TRIP AND PROTECTION SYSTEM MODELS

Reactor trip is latched when any enabled condition becomes true (high flux, high P, low P, low flow, manual, high T_clad). Once latched it remains active. Scram rod insertion follows the ramp model in the reactivity section.

ESFAS actuation: I_ESFAS = I_low_P OR I_high_containment_P OR I_manual_SI. Each injection source becomes available after t_ESFAS plus its specified delay.

Trips, SI actuation, PORV opening, accumulator start, and containment spray actuation are all recorded with their first-occurrence time (latched event reporting). These form the sequence-of-events output.


# FLARECON CONTAINMENT RESPONSE MODEL

## Physical Scope

FLARECON is a single-node dry-containment response model. The containment free volume contains a well-mixed gas mixture of dry air, steam, and hydrogen. Tracked quantities: gas pressure, gas temperature, gas composition (air, steam, hydrogen), wall/liner heat removal, floor/sump level, condensation, spray heat removal, optional sump cooling, and hydrogen flammability status.

State vector: [M_air, M_steam, M_H2, U_gas, T_wall, T_floor, M_sump]

Driven by FLARE RCS output: break mass flow, break enthalpy, hydrogen generation rate.

## Species Mass Balances

Dry air: dM_air/dt = 0 (constant unless leakage activated)
Steam: dM_s/dt = m_s,in - m_cond + m_spray,evap - m_leak,s
Hydrogen: dM_H2/dt = m_H2,in - m_leak,H2
Sump: dM_sump/dt = m_l,in + m_cond - m_spray,evap - m_drain

## Gas Equation of State

Air and hydrogen: ideal gas. P_air = M_air * R_air * T_g / V_g; P_H2 = M_H2 * R_H2 * T_g / V_g.
Steam partial pressure limited by saturation: P_s <= P_sat(T_g). Excess steam condenses.
Total pressure: P_tot = P_air + P_s + P_H2.

## Energy Balance

dU_g/dt = H_in - Q_wall - Q_floor - Q_spray - Q_LHSI - H_leak

H_in = m_break * h_break (source enthalpy from RCS)

## Cooling Models

Wall/liner: Q_wall = h_wall * A_wall * (T_g - T_wall). Wall thermal mass modeled with 1-D conduction into concrete.
Wall condensation: m_cond = K_cond * A_wall * max(0, P_s - P_sat(T_wall)); Q_cond = m_cond * h_fg
Spray: Q_spray = eta_spray * m_spray * cp_w * (T_g - T_spray)
Sump cooling: Q_LHSI = prescribed function of time

## Hydrogen Flammability Assessment

Nonflammable: y_H2 < 0.04 or y_steam >= 0.55
Deflagration region: 0.04 <= y_H2 <= 0.75 and y_steam < 0.55
Detonation screening: 0.18 <= y_H2 <= 0.59 and y_steam < 0.35

FLARECON reports flammability status as a screening indicator only. It does not simulate ignition, flame acceleration, combustion pressure spikes, or deflagration-to-detonation transition.

## Coupling Limitation

One-way coupling: FLARE RCS output drives FLARECON. Containment back-pressure does not feed back into the RCS break-flow calculation (P_back,RCS = P_back,0 throughout). This is a known limitation.


# UNCERTAINTY ANALYSIS

## Framework

The UA module (flare_ua.py) performs a Monte Carlo sampling loop. N statistically independent FLARE simulations are executed, each with a different realization of selected uncertain inputs. For a scalar figure of merit Y = g(x), the N samples Y_i = g(x_i) are collected and sorted.

For Wilks use, samples must be independent realizations from a fixed model with a fixed uncertainty catalogue. Changing the model or catalogue during the run invalidates the non-parametric interpretation.

## Sampling

Uniform[a, b]: x = a + (b - a) * U, U ~ Uniform(0,1)
Normal(mu, sigma): x = mu + sigma * Z, Z ~ N(0,1)
Lognormal: x = exp(mu + sigma * Z)
Triangular(a, b, mode): via numpy.random.triangular

Invalid parameters (lower >= upper for uniform; sigma <= 0 for normal) are caught before the run. Variables with invalid parameters are held constant at their base value and a warning is printed to the log.

## Order Statistics and Wilks Tolerance Limit

Sorted outputs: Y_1 <= Y_2 <= ... <= Y_N. The m-th largest value (m=1 is the maximum) provides a one-sided tolerance limit.

Wilks confidence beta for the m-th largest of N independent samples at coverage gamma:
  beta = 1 - I_gamma(m, N - m + 1)   [incomplete beta function ratio; distribution-free]

## First-Order Wilks (m=1)

beta = 1 - gamma^N. For a 95/95 tolerance limit (gamma=0.95, beta=0.95): N >= 59.
The maximum of 59 independent samples is a one-sided 95/95 upper tolerance limit.

## Higher-Order Wilks

For m=2 and 95/95: N=93. For m=3: N=124. For m=10: N=311.
The choice of m must be made before the analysis to avoid selection bias.

## Testing Method for Fixed Acceptance Limits

If all 59 cases pass simultaneously (all figures of merit below their regulatory limits), the joint pass probability is at least 95% with 95% confidence. This is the Wallis-Nutt testing method. A limited-failure variant uses the binomial formula to accommodate m > 0 allowed failures.

## FLARE UA Results Tab — Three Sub-Tabs

FOM Distribution: time-series overlay for all N samples, histogram of the scalar FOM, top-10 ordered values table, Wilks confidence summary for m = 1 through 10 at gamma = 95%, empirical CDF. When FLARECON outputs are present: containment time-series overlays and scalar summary tiles with H2 colour coding at 4 vol% LFL and 8 vol% detonable threshold.

Importance Analysis: stepwise multiple-regression decomposition of output variance (see below). Student-t threshold control, four KPI tiles (sigma_o, sqrt(Sum Var), ratio, residual sigma), stepwise regression table, tornado bar chart, correlation bar chart, per-contributor scatter plots. FLARECON importance analysis appears below when containment results are present.

Ordered Statistic: top-20 ordered values plot with selectable m, three summary tiles (m-th value, Wilks beta with pass/fail colour, maximum), beta vs m curve, minimum-n reference table. FLARECON ordered statistics appear below when containment results are present.


## Importance Analysis (Section 8.10 — Martin, 2011)

### Purpose

Identifies which sampled input parameters drive the calculated output uncertainty by decomposing the total output variance into contributions from individual uncertainty contributors.

Theory reference: R.P. Martin, "Quantifying Phenomenological Importance in Best-Estimate Plus Uncertainty Analyses," Nuclear Technology, Vol. 175, pp. 652-662, September 2011.

### Variance Decomposition Framework

Under ANOVA assumptions (independently sampled inputs):
  Var[x_o] = sum_{i=1}^{n} Var[x_{o,i}]

where x_{o,i} is the component of the output that varies with the i-th uncertainty contributor. This is a special case of the Sobol/Saltelli generalized variance decomposition.

### Sample Correlation Coefficient

r_xy = sum[(x - mu_x)(y - mu_y)] / [sqrt(sum[(x-mu_x)^2]) * sqrt(sum[(y-mu_y)^2])]

Positive r: output increases with input. Negative r: output decreases with input (e.g. a negative correlation between post-CHF HTC and PCT means better cooling reduces temperature, as physically expected).

### Student-t Stopping Threshold

t_p = r * sqrt(n - 2) / sqrt(1 - r^2)
r_min = t_p / sqrt(n - 2 + t_p^2)

FLARE default: two-sided 97.5% confidence (equivalent in breadth to one-sided 95%, consistent with the Wilks tolerance level). At t_{0.975} = 2.00 and n = 59: r_min = 0.256.

The regression terminates when max|r| < r_min among all remaining inputs. The parameter that caused the stop is reported as a terminal diagnostic row (not an accepted contributor).

### Stepwise Multiple-Regression Algorithm

At each step k:
1. Identify input x_k with largest |r| to current residual output
2. Fit polynomial (degree 1 or 2) to {x_k, residual} by least squares
3. Variance contribution: Var[x_{o,k}]|est = variance of fitted values at step k
4. Update residual by subtracting fitted values
5. Repeat until max|r| < r_min

Polynomial degree selected by adjusted R^2 (penalizes extra parameters, prevents overfitting with small samples). Delta_sigma_k = sqrt(Var[x_{o,k}]|est) reported in physical units of the figure of merit.

### Convolution Estimate and Ratio to Actual

sigma_o|est = sqrt( sum_{i=1}^{m} Var[x_i]|est )   [Eq. 11 of Martin 2011]
Ratio = sigma_o|est / sigma_o                        [Eq. 12 of Martin 2011]

Ratio >= 0.90 is satisfactory. Ratio < 0.90 suggests additional contributors below the stopping threshold or nonlinear interactions. Increasing n or lowering the confidence level may reveal them.

### Validation

Martin (2011) validated against the AREVA NP RLBLOCA three-loop PWR 59-calculation sample problem. Dominant contributors identified: break area, power shape, post-CHF dispersed-flow film-boiling HTC, global peaking factor. Convolution estimate 218.6 deg F vs actual sigma 239.5 deg F; ratio 0.912. Consistent with TPG expert PIRT rankings.


# SOURCE-TERM AND RADIOLOGICAL DOSE MODELS (NOTBADTRAD)

## Purpose

NOTBADTRAD is FLARE's reduced-order source-term and radiological consequence module providing transparent screening-level dose estimates. It is not RADTRAD, MACCS, HABIT, or ARCON96.

## Radionuclide Group Structure (9 groups, aligned with RG 1.183 / NUREG-1465)

- Noble gases (Xe, Kr): cloud dose only, no removal credit
- Halogens (I, Br): thyroid and TEDE; filtration/deposition may be credited
- Alkali metals (Cs, Rb): aerosol
- Tellurium group (Te, Sb, Se): semi-volatile aerosol
- Barium/strontium (Ba, Sr): lower-volatility aerosol
- Noble metals (Ru, Rh, Pd, Mo, Tc, Co): low-volatility aerosol
- Cerium group (Ce, Pu, Np): refractory
- Lanthanides (La, Zr, Nd, Eu, Nb, Pm, Pr, Sm, Y, Cm, Am): refractory
- Uranium/actinides: very low-volatility refractory (severe-event table only)

## DBA Release Fractions (RG 1.183 / NUREG-1465 aligned)

Group            Gap release    Early in-vessel    Total
Noble gases      0.05           0.95               1.0000
Halogens         0.05           0.35               0.4000
Alkali metals    0.05           0.25               0.3000
Tellurium        0.00           0.05               0.0500
Ba/Sr            0.00           0.02               0.0200
Noble metals     0.00           0.0025             0.0025
Cerium group     0.00           0.0005             0.0005
Lanthanides      0.00           0.0002             0.0002

Released inventory: I_rel_g(t) = I_g,0 * (R_gap * f_gap(t) + R_EIV * f_EIV(t))

## Severe Core-Melt Release Fractions

Interpolated between DBA and severe-event targets using E_mean (mean oxidizing rod ECR as a fraction):
  R_severe,g = R_DBA,g + E_mean * (R_BDBE,g - R_DBA,g)

Severe-event targets: noble gases 1.000, halogens 0.750, alkali metals 0.750, tellurium 0.300, Ba/Sr 0.120, noble metals 0.005, cerium 0.005, lanthanides 0.005, uranium/actinides 0.001.

## Transport and Dose

Single-compartment: dA_g/dt = S_g(t) - (lambda_leak + lambda_decay + lambda_dep + lambda_spray + lambda_filter) * A_g

Atmospheric dispersion factor chi/Q applied per receptor. TEDE = sum_g [Q_leaked,g * (chi/Q)_r * (BR * DCF_inh,g + DCF_cloud,g)].

Default: no decay credit, no deposition credit (conservative regulatory basis).

## Iodine Spike Model

Equilibrium spike (scram or PORV opening): Q_equil = 500 * a_coolant * M_primary
Accident spike (DNB or gap release): Q_accid = 335 * a_coolant * M_primary
Combined: f_ISP = min[(Q_equil + Q_accid) / I_Halogens, 1.0]

## Source-Term Inventory Model

Group inventories scale from fuel geometry using fission-product yield data calibrated to NUREG-1465 Table 2 EOC inventory. The rod count used for inventory is controlled by nbt_n_fuel_rods, which defaults to N_pins (the thermal-hydraulic rod count). If nbt_n_fuel_rods is explicitly set to a value different from N_pins, a warning is printed. All group inventories can be overridden individually via nbt_inv_{Group}_Ci parameters.

## Dose Screening Limits

EAB: 25 rem TEDE (10 CFR 50.34 / 50.67)
LPZ: 25 rem TEDE
Control room: 5 rem TEDE


# RISK ASSESSMENT TOOL

The Risk Tool maps FLARE cases onto a Frequency-Consequence (F-C) chart. Event frequency is user-assigned; consequence is EAB TEDE from NOTBADTRAD. The F-C boundary follows NEI 18-04 Rev. 1 LMP-style design objectives.

F-C boundary (log-log interpolated):
  EAB dose [rem]  /  Frequency [events/yr]
  0.01            /  10           (screening region)
  0.1             /  1            (AOO to DBE transition)
  1.0             /  0.1          (DBE upper range)
  1.0             /  0.01         (DBE lower range)
  25              /  1e-4         (BDBE upper range)
  1000            /  5e-7         (BDBE lower range)

Logarithmic margin M_i = log10(F_lim(C_i) / F_i). Positive = below boundary.

The F-C chart is a screening tool, not a substitute for a full PRA. Both deterministic acceptance criteria and F-C screening must be satisfied in a complete safety evaluation.

An AI Risk Narrative can be generated. It uses the same section-by-section generation approach as the PWR Simulator, with the same 21-level detail slider.


# AI EVENT NARRATIVE

Narrative generation is section-by-section: each section is a separate API call displayed as it completes. A progress bar and status indicator are shown; the page refreshes to the final stored report after completion.

The Narrative detail slider has 21 levels (0.00 to 1.00 in 0.05 increments). Target word count ranges from approximately 300 words at 0.00 to approximately 5,300 words at 1.00.

Section plan by detail level:
- 0.00: plant response overview only
- 0.15: adds safety system and thermal-hydraulic response
- 0.25: adds fuel integrity and core thermal response
- 0.40: adds containment response (when FLARECON data present)
- 0.55: adds source term and radiological consequences (when data present)
- 0.75: adds review observations and model caveats
- 1.00: all sections at maximum word target

For benign cases with no fuel damage, FLARE condenses to a short three-section structure regardless of the slider. Sections covering fuel integrity or source term are suppressed when their data are absent or zero.

The AI Uncertainty Narrative in the UA tool follows the same architecture and additionally draws on the stepwise regression table, Wilks order-statistic results for m = 1, 2, and 3, and the sampled input matrix.
